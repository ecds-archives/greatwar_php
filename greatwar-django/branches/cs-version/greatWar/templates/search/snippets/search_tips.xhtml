Search tips:
  • Truncation: "level" (with out quotes) finds "state-level", "Cleveland", and "level"
  • Exact phrase: Enter "treatment program" (with quotes) only finds "treatment program" and "level" only finds "level"
  • Multiple words finds documents containing all words entered.
  • Character case is ignored.
What are search fields, actually?

This seach engine searches through descriptions of individual ICPSR datasets, as provided by the ICPSR itself. To describe its data holdings, the ICPSR uses a "metadata standard" known as the DDI http://www.ddialliance.org/. These descriptions consist of different "fields" or categories of information about the datasets - e.g. the countries and time periods covered by the data, who gathered the data, and so on. This search engine uses the following fields/categories from the descriptions to search for datasets:


Field Name
Title + Study No. The name and ICPSR study number for a data collection
Summary The ICPSR's overview of the data collection, such as to the general topics covered by the data and the reasons why the data were gathered
Principal Investigator The individual(s) or organization who gathered the data - the "authors" of the data.
Subject Terms A detailed list of the subjects/topics covered by the data.
Geographic Coverage The countries and geographic areas included in the data.
Time Period The range of dates/years covered by the data.
